Meme coins, tokens, & NFT's - the wild side of crypto

Not all cryptocurrencies are created equal. While Bitcoin and Ethereum focus on security and decentralization, the crypto world has expanded into meme coins, tokens, and NFTs— each with unique use cases, risks, and opportunities. This sector will explore what these assets are, how they work, and why they continue to make waves in the market.

What are meme coins?

Meme coins are cryptocurrencies inspired by internet culture, jokes, or viral trends. While some started as a joke, they have gained massive popularity, often fueled by social media hype and celebrity endorsements.

Key characteristics:

  • ✓ Usually have no intrinsic value or major utility.
  • ✓ Prices are driven by community hype, speculation, and social media influence.
  • ✓ Extremely volatile—can skyrocket or crash overnight.

Most famous meme coins:

  • • Dogecoin (DOGE): The original meme coin, created in 2013 as a joke. Supported by Elon Musk and used for tipping and payments.
  • • Shiba Inu (SHIB):A Dogecoin competitor that launched in 2020, with its own ecosystem of DeFi and NFT projects.
  • • Pepe Coin (PEPE):Based on the popular internet meme, this coin saw huge speculative growth.
  • • Floki Inu (FLOKI):Named after Elon Musk’s dog, combining meme culture with utility projects.
  • • Store of Value:Many investors refer to Bitcoin as digital gold, as it is seen as a hedge against inflation.

Example: In 2021, Dogecoin surged over 12,000% after Elon Musk repeatedly tweeted about it.

Risk Warning: Meme coins are highly speculative and often lack strong fundamentals. Many pump and dump schemes have occurred in the meme coin space

What are crypto tokens?

While cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum have their own blockchains, tokens are built on top of existing blockchains like Ethereum or Binance Smart Chain.

Types of Crypto Tokens:

  • ✓ Utility Tokens:Give users access to services (e.g., BNB for Binance fees, UNI for Uniswap governance).
  • ✓ Governance Tokens:Allow holders to vote on project decisions (e.g., AAVE, COMP).
  • ✓ Security Tokens:Represent real-world assets like stocks or real estate.
  • ✓ Stablecoins:Pegged to a stable asset (e.g., USDT, USDC).

Most popular tokens:

  • • Binance Coin (BNB): Powers the Binance exchange and Binance Smart Chain.
  • • Chainlink (LINK):A Connects real-world data to blockchain applications.
  • • Uniswap (UNI):Gives governance power to users of the Uniswap exchange.
  • • ApeCoin (APE):Used in the Bored Ape Yacht Club ecosystem and NFT-related projects.

Example: Tokens like Uniswap’s UNI allow holders to vote on protocol changes, giving them influence over the project’s future direction.

Risk Warning: Some tokens are well-established, while others are speculative and subject to manipulation.

What are NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens)?

NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) represent unique digital assets that cannot be replicated. Unlike cryptocurrencies (which are interchangeable), each NFT is one of a kind and is stored on a blockchain.

Key Characteristics:

  • ✓ Digital Ownership:Verifies who owns digital art, music, collectibles, and virtual items.
  • ✓ Built on Blockchains:Allow holders to vote on project decisions (e.g., AAVE, COMP).
  • ✓ Most NFTs exist on Ethereum (ETH), Solana (SOL), and Polygon (MATIC).Represent real-world assets like stocks or real estate.
  • ✓ Used in Multiple Industries:Gaming, real estate, music, fashion, and more.

Most famous NFT collections:

  • • Bored Ape Yacht Club (BAYC): A collection of rare digital apes owned by celebrities.
  • • CryptoPunks: One of the first-ever NFT projects, dating back to 2017.
  • • Azuki & Pudgy Penguins:Popular NFT art collections with strong communities.
  • • NBA Top Shot: Officially licensed NFT highlights of basketball moments.

NFT use cases beyond art:

  • ✓ Gaming:Players own in-game assets that they can trade (e.g., Axie Infinity, Decentraland).
  • ✓ Virtual Real Estate:People buy land in the metaverse (e.g., Sandbox, Otherside).
  • ✓ Music & Entertainment:Musicians sell songs and concert tickets as NFTs.
  • ✓ Used in Multiple Industries:Gaming, real estate, music, fashion, and more.

Example: A single CryptoPunk NFT sold for $11.8 million at auction in 2021.

Risk Warning: Many NFTs are highly speculative, and the market can be unpredictable.

How to buy & trade meme coins, tokens, and NFTs

Buying Meme Coins & Tokens:

  • ✓ Step 1: Get a crypto wallet (MetaMask, Phantom, Trust Wallet).
  • ✓ Step 2: Buy Ethereum (ETH) or Solana (SOL) on an exchange.
  • ✓ Step 3: Connect your wallet to an NFT marketplace (OpenSea, Magic Eden, Rarible).
  • ✓ Step 4: Choose an NFT and buy it.

Key Characteristics:

  • ✓ Digital Ownership:Verifies who owns digital art, music, collectibles, and virtual items.
  • ✓ Built on Blockchains:Allow holders to vote on project decisions (e.g., AAVE, COMP).
  • ✓ Most NFTs exist on Ethereum (ETH), Solana (SOL), and Polygon (MATIC).Represent real-world assets like stocks or real estate.
  • ✓ Used in Multiple Industries:Gaming, real estate, music, fashion, and more.

Pro Tip: Always verify that you’re buying from legitimate sources to avoid scams.

Are meme coins, tokens, and NFTs worth it?

Each category has different risks and potential rewards:

  • Meme Coins:High risk, speculative, driven by hype and social media trends.
  • Utility Tokens:Some have real use cases, others are purely speculative.
  • NFTs:Unique ownership and cultural significance, but volatile in price.
  • ✓ Some traders profit from short-term speculation on these assets.
  • ✓ Others focus on long-term investments in well-established projects.
  • ✓ Always research before investing, as these markets are prone to rapid changes.

Final thoughts - the future of meme coins, tokens & NFTs

  • ✓ Meme coins have shown explosive growth, but their future depends on community and hype.
  • ✓ Crypto tokens play a crucial role in DeFi, governance, and blockchain ecosystems.
  • ✓ NFTs continue to evolve beyond collectibles, integrating into gaming, music, and real-world assets.

Understanding these assets helps traders navigate the ever-changing crypto landscape with knowledge, not just speculation.
